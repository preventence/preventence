## What Is Preventive Maintenance?

**Preventive maintenance (PM)** is a proactive maintenance approach where equipment is serviced at **planned intervals** to prevent failures before they occur.
Instead of waiting for machines to break down, preventive maintenance focuses on **early detection, routine inspections, and scheduled servicing**.

### Common preventive maintenance activities include:

- Routine inspections  
- Cleaning and lubrication  
- Calibration and alignment  
- Replacement of worn components  
- Safety and performance checks  

The goal is simple:

> **Fix small issues before they turn into expensive failures.**

## Why Preventive Maintenance Is Important for Industries

### 1. Reduced Unplanned Downtime

Unexpected breakdowns halt production and cause financial losses. Preventive maintenance minimizes failures and keeps operations running smoothly.

### 2. Longer Equipment Lifespan

Machines that receive regular care last significantly longer, reducing frequent replacements and lowering capital expenditure.

### 3. Lower Maintenance Costs

Planned maintenance is far more cost-effective than emergency repairs, overtime labor, and urgent spare-part purchases.

### 4. Improved Workplace Safety

Early detection of faults reduces safety risks and ensures compliance with industrial and regulatory standards.

### 5. Better Planning and Predictability

Maintenance teams know **what to do, when to do it, and what resources are required**, eliminating confusion and inefficiency.

## What Is CMMS-Based Preventive Maintenance?

A **CMMS (Computerized Maintenance Management System)** digitizes and automates preventive maintenance activities.

With a CMMS in place, industries can:

- Automate maintenance schedules  
- Generate digital work orders  
- Track complete asset history  
- Manage spare parts inventory  
- Monitor technician performance  
- Analyze downtime and maintenance costs  

A CMMS turns preventive maintenance into a **structured, traceable, and scalable process**.
